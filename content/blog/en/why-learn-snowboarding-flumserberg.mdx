If you live in the north of Switzerland and you've been meaning to learn, you don't need to drive four hours to the big-name resorts. Flumserberg is right there, and for a first season it's honestly better. Here's why I teach here.

## It's close

Flumserberg sits in the Heidiland region, easy to reach from Zürich and St. Gallen — around an hour by car, and doable by train and bus too. That matters more than it sounds. The biggest thing that kills a beginner's progress isn't talent, it's distance: if the mountain is a half-day trip, you go three times a season. If it's an hour away, you go ten times, and ten short days beat three long ones every winter.

## The beginner zone is free

This is the part people don't believe at first. The beginner area is free and open to everyone — no lift pass needed to start. That's a big deal. It means your first day costs you nothing in lift tickets, and there's genuinely no excuse not to try. We stay in that zone while you find your edges, and only once you're linking turns do we head up the mountain, where you'll buy your own day pass and I'll point you to the right counter.

It's also why I can keep first lessons honest: you're not paying for a mountain you can't use yet.

## The terrain suits learning

Some resorts throw you onto steep, icy, crowded runs from the first lift. Flumserberg has wide, gentle slopes near the bottom that are exactly what you want when you're learning to trust an edge. Room to make mistakes, soft enough to fall on, quiet enough that you're not dodging traffic. As you improve, the mountain opens up — long blue and red runs, proper terrain to carve, and a park when you're ready for freestyle. It grows with you across a season instead of overwhelming you on day one.

## It's where I started

Flumserberg is the first mountain I worked on as an instructor in Switzerland, so I know it the way you only know a place you've ridden hundreds of times — where the good snow sits in the afternoon, which runs stay quiet, where a nervous first-timer can find space. That local knowledge is half of what you're paying a coach for.

## The view doesn't hurt

On a clear day you're looking out over the Walensee far below, with the Churfirsten ridge across the valley. It's the kind of view that makes the early falls feel worth it. Snowboarding is partly about getting good and partly about being somewhere that pulls you out of your week — Flumserberg does both.

## How to start

You don't need much: warm waterproof layers, a rental board and boots (I'll point you to the shop), and a date. We meet at the COLORS door, stay in the free zone until your nerves settle, and go from there.

The season runs roughly late November to April. The early and late weeks are quieter and often the nicest to learn in. Pick a date, and if you've got questions first, just call me: +41 76 638 18 70.
